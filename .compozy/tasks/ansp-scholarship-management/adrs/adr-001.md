# ADR-001: Faithful Digital Translation of Existing Paper Workflow

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-16

## Context

ANSP needs to digitize its scholarship application process for the 2026 school year. The current workflow is paper-based: families fill out a 5-page form, submit physical documents, and the school committee reviews and decides manually. Four HTML prototypes already exist (login, 6-step form, 3-view admin panel, design system) that closely mirror the paper process. The committee has 2-5 members. Families do not need online status tracking — communication happens offline.

Three product approaches were considered: (A) faithful digital translation matching the prototypes, (B) enhanced committee workflow with assignment/comments/audit trail, and (C) full CEBAS compliance-ready platform with auto-analysis and compliance dashboards.

## Decision

Adopt Approach A: Faithful Digital Translation. Build the system to closely match the existing HTML prototypes, digitizing the paper workflow with minimal additions. Committee members get individual logins to a shared admin interface. The form opens/closes based on Ano Letivo dates. Documents are uploaded to cloud storage and downloadable by admins.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Enhanced Committee Workflow

- **Description**: Adds application assignment, internal comments, and audit trail for committee collaboration
- **Pros**: Enables committee to divide work without verbal coordination; audit trail supports compliance
- **Cons**: ~40% more development effort; requires user management and permissions from day one
- **Why rejected**: MVP should validate the digital workflow first; collaboration features can be layered on later without rework

### Alternative 2: Full Compliance-Ready Platform

- **Description**: Adds per-capita income auto-calculation, PROUNI threshold flagging, parecer social workflow, compliance dashboard
- **Pros**: Purpose-built for CEBAS audit readiness; reduces manual errors
- **Cons**: 2-3x development effort; compliance features may duplicate existing school systems; premature for MVP
- **Why rejected**: Over-engineers the MVP; the school may handle compliance reporting through other channels

## Consequences

### Positive

- Fastest path to a working system for the 2026 enrollment cycle
- Low risk — prototypes serve as a validated spec
- Families and staff experience a familiar workflow in digital form
- Foundation supports incremental enhancement in later phases

### Negative

- Committee members cannot assign or annotate applications within the system — coordination stays verbal
- No automated income analysis — committee calculates per-capita income manually
- No built-in audit trail for CEBAS compliance documentation

### Risks

- If the committee finds verbal coordination insufficient at scale, the assignment/comments features from Approach B may be needed sooner than planned. Mitigation: design the data model to accommodate these features without migration.

## Implementation Notes

- All UI follows the existing HTML prototypes in `design/`
- Authentication: individual accounts for 2-5 committee members (shared admin role)
- Cloud database for application data, cloud storage for document uploads
- Form availability controlled by Ano Letivo start/end dates
